# Federal & national-scope funding opportunities

Researched 2026-04-27. All program details should be re-verified against the funder's own page before any application draft — eligibility wording and amounts shift quietly between intake cycles.

---

### CIRA Community Investment Program (Canadian Internet Registration Authority)

- **URL:** https://www.cira.ca/en/grants/ ; portal at https://cipapplication.cira.ca/
- **Eligibility:** Registered charities, not-for-profits, and academics affiliated with a Canadian university/college. Org must be Canadian-based; project must benefit Canadians. Charitable status is **not** required — incorporated NPOs qualify. Required docs include incorporation proof, board list, audited financial statements, operating budget, three letters of support.
- **Award size:** Typical $25,000–$50,000; max $100,000.
- **Cycle:** Annual single intake. The 2026 cycle closed at 2 p.m. ET on **March 18, 2026** (just missed). Next intake will be early 2027.
- **Fit:** **Highest a-priori fit on this list.** Funds projects in four streams — Infrastructure, Digital Literacy, Cybersecurity, Community Leadership — and CPD plausibly slots into Infrastructure (public dataset + tooling) or Community Leadership (civic transparency). CIRA has previously funded civic-tech of similar shape.

### SSHRC Connection Grants — Joint Initiative for Digital Citizen Research (SSHRC + Canadian Heritage)

- **URL:** https://www.canada.ca/en/canadian-heritage/services/online-disinformation/joint-initiative-digital-citizen-research/connection-grants.html
- **Eligibility:** Open to institutions, individual applicants, and teams. The applicant must be a Canadian postsecondary institution **or a not-for-profit organization holding institutional eligibility with SSHRC** (a one-time vetting process — contact SSHRC's Institutional Eligibility team ≥10 business days before deadline). 50% matching cash/in-kind required.
- **Award size:** $10,000–$50,000 over 1 year for outreach activities (higher amounts considered with justification).
- **Cycle:** Quarterly: **Feb 2, May 1, Aug 4, Nov 2**, all 8 p.m. ET. Realistic next target is **Aug 4, 2026**.
- **Fit:** Excellent. Mandate explicitly covers "impacts of online disinformation on democratic institutions and elections" and "knowledge mobilization" — CPD's Hansard search engine is a knowledge-mobilization platform for democratic data.

### SSHRC Partnership Engage Grants

- **URL:** https://sshrc-crsh.canada.ca/en/funding/opportunities/partnership-engage-grants/2026/competition.aspx
- **Eligibility:** **Requires an academic co-applicant.** Only an eligible Canadian postsecondary institution can administer the funds. A non-profit can be the partner organization and source of the research question; a U of A / MacEwan / Athabasca researcher would be the lead. The NPO can request a salary research allowance to release a co-applicant from organizational duties.
- **Award size:** $10,000–$50,000, 1 year.
- **Cycle:** Quarterly: **Mar 16, Jun 15, Sep 15, Dec 15** (all 8 p.m. ET). Next reachable: **Jun 15, 2026** if you can line up a faculty co-applicant in the next ~6 weeks.
- **Fit:** Strong if you can find an academic partner. Natural fits: U of A Political Science (Hansard / parliamentary studies), Computing Science (NLP/IR research on a 1.7M-doc multilingual corpus), Athabasca Centre for Distance Education (accessibility). The Hansard corpus + Qwen3 embeddings is a publishable research artefact.

### Mozilla Foundation Democracy x AI Cohort (international, Canada-eligible)

- **URL:** https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/what-we-do/grantmaking/incubator/democracy-ai-cohort/
- **Eligibility:** Open to global applicants. Must have legal right to receive funds from Mozilla (US 501(c)(3)) — Canadian NPOs have done this routinely. Open-source code (or roadmap to open-source) is **mandatory** — CPD already meets this. Working tech with demonstrated traction required; must be able to commit to a 12-month engagement.
- **Award size:** **$50,000 USD** per project (10 selected); top 2 performers advance to a "Sustain Track" worth an additional **$250,000 USD**.
- **Cycle:** 2026 intake closed Mar 16, 2026. Next round expected late 2026 / early 2027.
- **Fit:** **Best-named-on-the-tin fit on this list.** "AI tools that protect democracy" with open-source preference: CPD's semantic search over Hansard using a self-hosted Qwen3 model is a textbook example. Scoring criteria explicitly favour traction (1.7M+ speeches indexed, public site live). **Note:** PolyForm Noncommercial may need a sub-licensing carve-out for the Mozilla cohort which leans OSI-strict; confirm before applying.

### Canadian Heritage Digital Citizen Contribution Program (PCH)

- **URL:** https://www.canada.ca/en/canadian-heritage/services/online-disinformation/digital-citizen-contribution-program.html
- **Eligibility:** NPOs (national, provincial, community), professional associations, Indigenous organizations. Charitable status is **not** required.
- **Award size:** Historically up to ~$250,000 per project (3-year terms).
- **Cycle:** **No active intake.** The last call closed Nov 28, 2023; funding decisions extended to Mar 31, 2026. Program is flagged for "modification and reduction" under the federal Comprehensive Expenditure Review. **Watch list only.**
- **Fit:** Mandate (countering disinformation, supporting democratic resilience) is on-target but the program is in limbo. Track for re-launch.

### Mitacs Accelerate (Mitacs, federally co-funded)

- **URL:** https://www.mitacs.ca/our-programs/accelerate/
- **Eligibility:** Non-profits qualify as **partner organizations** alongside a Canadian post-secondary institution and a graduate-student intern. Projects with NPO partners must demonstrate "economic or productivity orientation" — frame around dataset/tool productivity, not advocacy.
- **Award size:** $15,000 per 4–6 month internship; partner contributes $7,500 (Mitacs matches). Stack multiple internships for larger projects.
- **Cycle:** **Rolling.** Submit anytime; ~6–8 week review.
- **Fit:** Good for offloading specific deliverables (provincial Hansard parser, accessibility audit, French-language IR evaluation) onto a grad student. Same university partner you'd line up for Partnership Engage Grants can be reused here. Cheap way to get research labour without hiring.

### NRC Outreach Initiative (National Research Council)

- **URL:** https://nrc.canada.ca/en/support-technology-innovation/outreach-initiative-grants-contributions-program
- **Eligibility:** Non-profits and charities. Indigenous outreach stream available. Funds science/innovation **outreach activities** (conferences, workshops, symposia) — not core software development.
- **Award size:** Varies; modest ($5K–$50K range historically).
- **Cycle:** Semi-annual — **Feb 10** and **Oct 10** each year. Apply for events that start by Mar 31 of the following year.
- **Fit:** Niche. Could fund a 2026 workshop / hackathon around the CPD dataset (e.g., "Hands-on with the Canadian Hansard Corpus" — students, journalists, researchers). Not a development-cost lever.

### McConnell Foundation (national private foundation)

- **URL:** https://www.mcconnellfoundation.ca/apply/
- **Eligibility:** **Charitable status (or qualified-donee status) typically required** for direct grants. Currently the Foundation prioritizes proactive, invitation-only partnerships in three streams: Communities, Reconciliation, Climate. A "Canadian Democracy @ Work" initiative exists but is not a published open call.
- **Award size:** Historically $30K–$500K+; varies widely by partnership.
- **Cycle:** **Largely closed to cold applications.** Strategic invite-only.
- **Fit:** Mission-fit (democratic engagement, civic skills) is good but the access path is relationship-based. Best route: a warm intro through an existing McConnell grantee. **Watch list / relationship-building** rather than near-term apply.

1. **CIRA Community Investment Program** — highest a priori fit, mid-five-figure cheque, no charitable status needed, NPO-friendly. Begin assembling audited financials + letters of support for the next intake (early 2027). If incorporation as an NPO completes in 2026, you'll be ready Day 1.
2. **SSHRC + PCH Joint Initiative for Digital Citizen Research Connection Grants** — Aug 4, 2026 deadline gives a realistic prep runway. Need to start the SSHRC institutional-eligibility process now (≥10 business days lead time, but practically more for a brand-new NPO).
3. **Mitacs Accelerate** — rolling deadline + university partner + no charitable status needed = fastest to first dollar. Approach U of A Computing Science / Political Science with a defined deliverable (e.g., MB or YT Hansard pipeline as a 6-month intern project).

## Charitable-status gates (federal layer)

Programs **blocked** without CRA registered-charity status:
- McConnell Foundation direct grants typically require charity or qualified-donee status. Most large national foundations do.
- Trottier Family Foundation (registered charities + qualified donees only).

Programs **not blocked** by absence of charitable status (NPO / incorporation alone is enough):
- CIRA Community Investment Program
- SSHRC programs (with the institutional-eligibility vetting step)
- Mitacs Accelerate
- Mozilla Democracy x AI Cohort
- NRC Outreach Initiative
- PCH Digital Citizen Contribution Program (when it returns)

## Recommendation

Federal NPO incorporation alone unlocks the strongest near-term opportunities (CIRA, SSHRC Connection, Mitacs, Mozilla, NRC). Pursuing CRA charitable registration adds ~12–18 months of paperwork and ongoing T3010 compliance burden in exchange for access to private foundations (McConnell, Trottier, Inspirit org-stream). Defer charitable status until you have a McConnell- or Trottier-shaped opportunity in hand — the operational drag isn't worth it speculatively.
