# Edmonton & region funding opportunities

Researched 2026-04-27. Programs assessed for fit with a new Edmonton-headquartered civic-tech non-profit operating Canadian Political Data — a free, public semantic search engine over Canadian Hansard.

Charitable status (CRA T3010) is a hard gate for several of the strongest options — flagged inline. Verify all info directly before submitting.

---

### Community Investment Operating Grant — CIOG (City of Edmonton)

- **URL:** https://www.edmonton.ca/programs_services/funding_grants/community-investment-operating-grant
- **Eligibility:** Non-profit registered for at least 1 year (Alberta Society / non-profit company); **charitable status NOT required**. Min 75% of participants must be Edmonton residents. Excludes orgs holding 2+ years of unrestricted reserves. Eligible operating expenses must sit between $2,500 and $500,000.
- **Award size:** Award amounts not published; framed as a portion of operating expenses within the $2.5K–$500K eligibility band.
- **Cycle:** Annual. 2026 intake closed 14 Jan 2026; decisions end of April. **Next intake: ~Nov 2026 / Jan 2027.**
- **Fit:** Strong on paper for ongoing operating costs once CPD is incorporated > 1 year — but CIOG's stated priority is "social inclusion and equitable access to social services and active recreation/amateur sport," which is a stretch for a Hansard search engine. Frame against "equitable access to civic information / participation."

### Edmonton Community Foundation — Community Grants (ECF)

- **URL:** https://ecf.ca/grants/community-grants/
- **Eligibility:** **Must be a CRA-registered charity or qualified donee** — non-profit-only organizations do not qualify. Must serve Greater Edmonton Area (city + 4 surrounding counties) or Treaty 6 Indigenous donees.
- **Award size:** Up to **$50,000** (first-time recipients in the past 5 years) or up to **$80,000** (returning recipients).
- **Cycle:** Annual two-stage. 2027 cycle: Stage 1 opens early Feb, closes late Feb. Stage 2 closes mid-Apr; funds disburse after 1 Jul.
- **Fit:** ECF is the single largest civic funder in the region and explicitly funds project + operating + equipment. Vital-Signs-aligned themes (democratic participation, social inclusion of marginalized voices) are an excellent narrative fit. **Hard-gated on charitable status** — only worth pursuing if CPD pursues T3010 registration.

### Edmonton Community Foundation — Small Grants (ECF)

- **URL:** https://ecf.ca/grants/small-grants/
- **Eligibility:** **CRA-registered charity / qualified donee required.** Greater Edmonton Area service. One application per calendar year.
- **Award size:** Up to **$10,000**, for initiatives lasting ≤ 6 months.
- **Cycle:** Rolling intakes Mar 1 – Oct 1 each year. Next windows: 1–31 Mar, 1 Apr–31 May, 1 Jun–31 Jul, 1 Aug–1 Oct.
- **Fit:** Designed for capacity development and unanticipated needs — natural fit for a discrete deliverable like a provincial Hansard expansion sprint, accessibility audit, or screen-reader compliance work. Lower paperwork than the Community Grants program. Same charity-status gate.

### Edmonton Community Foundation — Vital Signs Grants (ECF)

- **URL:** https://ecf.ca/grants/ (Vital Signs program; no dedicated landing page)
- **Eligibility:** Non-profit serving Greater Edmonton with a **CRA charitable registration number**.
- **Award size:** Not publicly specified.
- **Cycle:** **No fixed deadline** — discretionary intake aligned to annual Vital Signs Report. Approach by inquiry.
- **Fit:** Strongest narrative fit of any ECF stream. Vital Signs measures community health vs. national benchmarks; CPD is, in effect, infrastructure for measuring democratic participation. Pitch as the data layer behind future Vital Signs editions, partnered with the Edmonton Social Planning Council (ESPC is ECF's existing Vital Signs research partner).

### TELUS Edmonton & Northern Alberta Community Board

- **URL:** https://www.friendlyfuture.com/en/foundation/community-boards/edmonton
- **Eligibility:** **CRA-registered charity or qualified donee.** Programs must serve Edmonton/Northern Alberta. Programs must support **youth up to age 29** with a focus on **health or education** outcomes.
- **Award size:** Up to **$20,000/year**. Edmonton board allocated $575K across 43 charities in 2025.
- **Cycle:** Annual. 2026 deadline was 30 Jan 2026; decisions in April. **Next deadline: late Jan 2027.**
- **Fit:** Workable only if scoped to a youth civic-education deliverable — e.g. a high-school / first-year-undergrad democratic-literacy module built on CPD's Hansard data, or accessibility features targeted at youth voters. Does not fit general operating. Charity status required.

### Edmonton Heritage Council — HCIP Heritage Project Grant

- **URL:** https://edmontonheritage.ca/grants/hcip
- **Eligibility:** Non-profits, registered charities, **and individuals** working on Edmonton-heritage projects (broadest applicant pool of anything on this list). Eligibility doc PDF should be confirmed for the project-grant stream specifically.
- **Award size:** Up to **$20,000**.
- **Cycle:** Applications open 17 Mar – 27 Apr each year. Annual; next intake ~Mar 2027.
- **Fit:** Plausible if framed as "preservation of Edmonton's political-record heritage" — e.g. ingesting and making searchable historical Edmonton MLA / city-council records, oral-history pieces. Requires public-sharing component (publication, exhibition, gathering) which CPD's website naturally provides. Edmonton-history scoping is the constraint; CPD's national scope would need a deliberately Edmonton-tied sub-project.

### Edmonton Heritage Council — HCIP Research & Development Grant

- **URL:** https://ehc.smapply.ca/prog/research_development_grant_hcip/
- **Eligibility:** Same broad pool as the Project Grant (non-profits, charities, individuals).
- **Award size:** Up to **$10,000**.
- **Cycle:** Annual within HCIP cycle. Confirm 2026 dates with grants@edmontonheritage.ca.
- **Fit:** Better fit than the Project Grant for early-stage scoping — e.g. R&D for an Edmonton civic-history corpus, feasibility for digitizing pre-Hansard council minutes. Lower amount, lower commitment, faster path to a first EHC relationship.

### Edmonton Regional Innovation Network — Community Initiatives Fund (ERIN)

- **URL:** https://www.edmontonrin.ca/community-initiatives
- **Eligibility:** For-profit OR non-profit. **Must be an ERIN member.** Initiative hosted in Edmonton Metro Region. Match-funded (applicant must contribute equal $). Does NOT fund business development or startup activities. Does NOT fund retroactively.
- **Award size:** Up to **$10,000**.
- **Cycle:** Annual spring call; historically opens mid-Feb, closes late-Mar.
- **Fit:** Marginal. The fund targets entrepreneur-ecosystem activities (events, workshops, networking). CPD could fit by hosting an "open civic data hackathon" or "Hansard API workshop for Edmonton developers" — that's a direct ecosystem deliverable, not infrastructure funding. Use as a relationship-building entry point, not core funding.

### Muttart Foundation — Charitable Sector Strengthening

- **URL:** https://www.muttart.org/charitable-activities/strengthening-the-charitable-sector/
- **Eligibility:** Edmonton-based foundation; funds work that helps "the voices of charities be heard at policy tables, nationally and regionally." **Charity status almost certainly required** (foundation grants typically restricted to qualified donees per CRA rules). Specific eligibility not posted publicly — by inquiry.
- **Award size:** Not published. Categories include bursaries, training grants, restructuring grants, occasional loans/lines of credit.
- **Cycle:** Not posted; relationship-driven application.
- **Fit:** Genuinely interesting longer-term fit. CPD's Hansard search is *infrastructure for charities to monitor policy debates* — exactly the policy-voice amplification Muttart describes. Best approached after a year of operating history + a clear story about how non-profits use the platform. Call 780-425-9616 or email for a scoping conversation.

### Stollery Charitable Foundation

- **URL:** https://www.stollerycharitablefoundation.org/
- **Eligibility:** Funds **qualified donees** (CRA charities) only, in Edmonton + Kamloops. Focus areas: health, education, social service, poverty alleviation, **human rights**.
- **Award size:** Not published; $52.8M distributed over 30 years.
- **Cycle:** Process not publicly documented; contact 780-470-5633.
- **Fit:** The "human rights" focus area is the relevant lever — frame CPD as access-to-information / right-to-know infrastructure (a recognized human-rights framing under Article 19 ICCPR). Charity status required.

## Quick wins — pursue these first

1. **ECF Small Grants** — single best fast-path option *if CPD becomes a registered charity*. Rolling intake, $10K cap, 6-month projects. Pitch a discrete deliverable: provincial Hansard expansion sprint, WCAG 2.2 accessibility audit + remediation, or a published open-data drop.
2. **EHC HCIP R&D Grant** — **individuals and non-profits both eligible**, no charity-status gate, $10K. Lowest barrier to a first Edmonton-region grant. Scope a small Edmonton political-heritage sub-project.
3. **ECF Community Grants** — the largest single check ($50K–$80K) realistic for a new org. Charity-status-gated; do not apply until T3010 is in hand. Plan now for Stage 1 in Feb 2027; build the ESPC/Vital Signs partnership angle in the meantime.

## Watch list — track but don't apply yet

- **City of Edmonton CIOG** — re-opens ~Nov 2026/Jan 2027. Will need ≥ 1 year of operating history and a credible "Edmonton residents served" framing. Worth a relationship call to grants@edmonton.ca first.
- **TELUS Edmonton Community Board** — re-opens ~Jan 2027. Only worth the lift if CPD spins up a youth-civic-education sub-program; otherwise skip.
- **Muttart Foundation** — no fixed cycle. Schedule a scoping call once CPD has 12 months of usage data showing charities/policy-shops as users. Strong long-game fit on the "charity voices at policy tables" framing.
- **Stollery Charitable Foundation** — same pattern as Muttart. Frame around access-to-information as a human-rights cause; charity status mandatory.
- **ERIN Community Initiatives Fund** — watch for the spring call (~Feb–Mar). Use only for an ecosystem-facing event (hackathon / open-data workshop), not core infra.

## Charity-status decision

At least 4 of the 10 strongest Edmonton-region programs (ECF Community, ECF Small, ECF Vital Signs, TELUS, Stollery, likely Muttart) are gated on **CRA charitable status**, not just non-profit incorporation. Pursuing T3010 registration alongside federal NPO incorporation roughly doubles the addressable Edmonton-region funding pool — but adds 12–18 months of paperwork and ongoing T3010 compliance burden.
