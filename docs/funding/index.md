# Funding research — Canadian Political Data

Researched 2026-04-27 across three levels of government for a hypothetical Edmonton-headquartered, federally-incorporated non-profit (BNKops as the operating org).

Three full reports, one per level:

- **[Federal & national](federal.md)** — CIRA, SSHRC, Mitacs, Mozilla, NRC, McConnell, etc.
- **[Alberta provincial](alberta.md)** — CIP, Max Bell, AREF, Calgary Foundation, Alberta Ecotrust, etc.
- **[Edmonton & region](edmonton.md)** — ECF, City of Edmonton CIOG, EHC HCIP, Muttart, Stollery, etc.

This index synthesizes the strategic decisions and gives a 12-month application calendar.

---

## The three strategic decisions to make first

### 1. Incorporate federally as a non-profit (yes, soon)

A federal NPO under the Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act unlocks the entire non-charity-gated funding lane. CPD's mission (public-interest civic data, free use) is a clean fit for the NPO purpose-statement requirement. Edmonton/BNKops as registered office; mission language framed around public access to legislative information.

### 2. Charitable status (T3010): defer for now, revisit in 12 months

The single most important strategic question. Roughly half the strongest opportunities require CRA charitable registration:

| Charity-gated (skip until T3010) | Non-charity-gated (apply as NPO) |
|---|---|
| ECF Community Grants ($50–80K) | **CIRA Community Investment ($25–100K)** |
| ECF Small Grants ($10K) | **SSHRC Connection ($10–50K)** |
| ECF Vital Signs (variable) | SSHRC Partnership Engage ($10–50K, w/ academic) |
| TELUS Community Board ($20K, youth-only) | **Mitacs Accelerate ($15K/intern, rolling)** |
| Calgary Foundation ($5–200K, Treaty 7 only) | **Mozilla Democracy x AI ($50K USD + $250K sustain)** |
| **Max Bell ($10–200K, best topical fit)** | **CIP Project-Based ($75K, Alberta-only, GoA)** |
| McConnell (varies, invite-only) | Alberta Ecotrust ($100K, climate-scoped) |
| Stollery (varies) | AREF ($25–250K, real-estate-scoped) |
| Muttart (varies) | EHC HCIP R&D ($10K, Edmonton heritage) |

**Recommendation:** Incorporate as NPO immediately, build a 12-month track record on the non-charity-gated side, then make the T3010 call once you have either (a) a McConnell- or Max-Bell-shaped opportunity in hand, or (b) a clear demand signal from charities/journalists/researchers using CPD that justifies the operational drag.

T3010 application is non-trivial (12–18 months processing, ongoing annual filing, programmatic restrictions on non-charitable activity). The drag is real; the upside is real too. Don't pursue it speculatively.

### 3. Find a university partner now

Three of the best near-term programs (SSHRC Partnership Engage, Mitacs Accelerate, NRC Outreach) need or strongly prefer a Canadian post-secondary co-applicant. The natural homes:

- **U of A Political Science** — Hansard/parliamentary studies, Canadian-government research. Look for faculty working on legislative behaviour, political communication, parliamentary procedure.
- **U of A Computing Science** — NLP/IR research on a 1.7M-doc multilingual corpus is a publishable artefact. Look for faculty in IR, NLP, or applied ML.
- **U of A Faculty of Law / Centre for Constitutional Studies** — democratic-institutions framing.
- **Athabasca University** — accessibility, distance education, open-data.
- **MacEwan University** — civic-engagement programming.

A single faculty contact can serve as co-applicant across multiple programs, so the up-front relationship cost amortizes.

### Months-1-to-3 priorities (do these now)

1. **Begin federal NPO incorporation.** The single highest-leverage administrative move — gates everything else.
2. **Open SSHRC institutional eligibility process.** ≥10 business days lead time minimum, practically longer for a brand-new NPO. Required for the Aug 4, 2026 Connection grant deadline.
3. **Cold outreach to U of A** — Political Science + Computing Science faculty. One faculty contact unlocks three programs.
4. **Prepare CIP Project-Based application** for May 15, 2026 (3 weeks away). $75K is a credible first cheque; the application is light relative to federal programs.

### Months 3-6 priorities

5. Apply for May / June / Aug deadlines (CIP, SSHRC PEG, SSHRC Connection).
6. Decide on T3010 trajectory based on early traction.
7. Start Max Bell LOI conversation (rolling, but lead time matters).

### Months 6-12 priorities

8. Build for the **early-2027 CIRA cycle** — get audited financials, 3 letters of support, infrastructure-stream narrative locked.
9. Lock in Mozilla Democracy x AI submission for the late-2026 / early-2027 round.
10. ECF Community Grants Stage 1 in Feb 2027 (T3010 must be in hand).

## Programs deliberately NOT recommended

A few funders surfaced in the research that I'm explicitly steering away from:

- **Suncor Energy Foundation** — industry-aligned, transparency-project antithetical to their priorities. Don't waste the application.
- **Calgary Foundation Community Grants** — geographically wrong (Treaty 7 only). Only viable through a Calgary partner.
- **Alberta Foundation for the Arts** — only fits if you collaborate with an artist on a specific cultural deliverable. Skip the core ask.
- **City of Edmonton CIOG** — operating-grant prerequisites (1-yr operating history + 75% Edmonton-resident participants) won't be met for a year+; revisit Q4 2026.
- **PCH Digital Citizen Contribution Program** — paused under federal expenditure review; track but don't plan around it.

## Political-feasibility note

GoA-administered programs (CIP, Civil Society Fund) carry meaningful UCP-tilt risk — current government messaging is hostile to "democratic-engagement" framing it associates with federal Liberal initiatives. Frame any GoA application around neutral civic-infrastructure language ("Albertans accessing the public record of their legislature," "open-data publication," "accessibility") — not "transparency / accountability / progressive civic-tech."

The non-government Alberta funders (Max Bell, AREF, Calgary Foundation, Ecotrust) carry no such risk and are the safer primary targets. Max Bell in particular *expects* policy-engagement framing.

Federal and Edmonton programs do not carry this risk in any structural way.

## Open questions worth thinking through

1. **PolyForm Noncommercial vs Mozilla's open-source bar** — verify whether the cohort accepts source-available licenses or requires OSI-approved. May need a permissive sub-license carve-out for Mozilla submissions.
2. **Fiscal sponsor for charity-gated funders** — if you decide to defer T3010 but want to apply for Max Bell or McConnell, who's the fiscal sponsor? Universities are the most common path; some Canadian civic-tech orgs (e.g. OpenNorth, Tides Canada) accept fiscal-sponsorship arrangements.
3. **BNKops vs CPD as the legal applicant** — if BNKops is the cooperative collective and CPD is one project under it, the funding application org is BNKops (with CPD as the named project). Some funders prefer single-project orgs; worth confirming pre-application that the BNKops/CPD relationship is clear.
