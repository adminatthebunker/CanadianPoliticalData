# Alberta provincial funding opportunities

Researched 2026-04-27. Programs assessed for fit with a new Alberta-headquartered civic-tech non-profit operating Canadian Political Data.

**Political-feasibility note up top:** the GoA-administered programs (CIP, Civil Society Fund) carry meaningful UCP-tilt risk — current government messaging is hostile to "democratic-engagement" framing it associates with federal Liberal initiatives. Frame any GoA application around neutral civic-infrastructure language, not transparency/accountability advocacy. The non-government Alberta funders (Max Bell, AREF, Calgary Foundation, Ecotrust) carry no such risk and are the safer primary targets.

---

### Community Initiatives Program — Project-Based (Government of Alberta, Culture & Status of Women)

- **URL:** https://www.alberta.ca/cip-project-based-grant
- **Eligibility:** Alberta-based non-profits or charities registered under provincial/federal acts. Charitable status not required. New non-profits qualify once registration completes. All project activity must occur in Alberta.
- **Award size:** Up to **$75,000** per project (max 50% of total project cost — applicant must match 1:1).
- **Cycle:** Three intakes per year. 2026 deadlines: **Jan 15, May 15, Sept 15**, with notification ~6 months later.
- **Fit:** Strong fit for a discrete deliverable — e.g., "expand Hansard semantic search to all Alberta legislative sessions back to 1972, publish AB-MLA roster as open data, accessibility audit." The 1:1 match is real but can be in-kind (BNKops staff time, donated infrastructure) per program guidelines.
- **Political-feasibility caveat: medium.** CIP sits inside a UCP-led ministry and the May 2025 reallocation announcement explicitly steered $8M to "food security, shelter, addictions" — civic-tech is not a stated priority. The Project-Based stream survived the reallocation and remains open, but framing matters: lead with "Albertans access their legislature's record" rather than "democratic-engagement / accountability watchdog."

### Alberta Real Estate Foundation — Investment Grants (AREF)

- **URL:** https://www.aref.ab.ca/funding/investment-grants
- **Eligibility:** Excludes individuals, for-profits, political/religious activity, and re-granters. Does *not* explicitly require charitable status or Alberta incorporation, but funded work must benefit Albertans. EOI required at least 3 weeks before each application deadline.
- **Award size:** Up to **$250,000** (Investment); up to $25,000 (Development); up to $10,000 (Sponsorship, year-round).
- **Cycle:** Two Investment intakes per year. 2026 deadlines: **March 24** (decisions June 9) and **August 25, 2026** (decisions Oct 27). Development + Sponsorship: rolling, 3–4 week turnaround.
- **Fit:** Tighter than it looks. AREF is real-estate-scoped — the natural pitch is the **infrastructure-sovereignty / website-hosting layer** (`websites`, `infrastructure_scans` tables), framed under "Built Environment" or "Real Estate Leadership / consumer education." The pitch: where municipal land-use decisions and zoning policy live in Hansard, AREF could fund the AB Hansard expansion + a property-policy filtered view. Not a fit for the core semantic-search project as a whole.

### Calgary Foundation — Community Grants (Calgary Foundation)

- **URL:** https://calgaryfoundation.org/charities/grants/community-grants/
- **Eligibility:** **Registered charity or qualified donee required.** Non-charity nonprofits may apply *in partnership* with a registered-charity sponsor. Geography: Calgary + Treaty 7 area (Banff, Canmore, Rocky View, Foothills, MD Big Horn/Kananaskis, Treaty 7 Nations) — **not** Alberta-wide despite the name.
- **Award size:** **$5,000 to $200,000.**
- **Cycle:** Fall 2026 intake opens late July, closes early September. (Spring intake also runs but exact 2026 dates pending.)
- **Fit:** Mission-aligned (civic engagement is in their published goals) but geographically tight: an Edmonton-headquartered org would need to demonstrate substantive Treaty-7-area benefit. Could work if you scope a deliverable to Calgary-area MLA / municipal-Hansard coverage, public-engagement workshops in Calgary, or a partnership with a Calgary-based charity sponsor.

### Max Bell Foundation — Civic Engagement & Democratic Institutions (Calgary-based, national reach)

- **URL:** https://www.maxbell.org/funding-priorities/ ; https://www.maxbell.org/apply-for-funding/
- **Eligibility:** **Registered charity or qualified donee required**; non-charity nonprofits must partner with a charity. Project must take place in Canada. Headquartered in Calgary and Alberta-rooted in history, but funds nationally — applying as an Edmonton org is fine.
- **Award size:** **$10,000 – $200,000**, 1–3 years. Foundation typically covers 30–60% of project cost; prefers co-funders.
- **Cycle:** **Rolling LOIs**, no fixed deadline. Board meets May / September / December — submit LOI ≥4 months ahead, full proposal ≥2 months ahead.
- **Fit:** **Best topical match in this entire list.** Their Civic Engagement & Democratic Institutions program area explicitly funds (a) resilient democratic institutions, (b) lowering barriers to civic engagement, (c) "improving public information and media environments." CPD's product description maps almost line-by-line. The catch is the charitable-status gate — pursue T3010 in parallel, or partner with a sponsor (e.g., a university policy school, a Canadian journalism foundation).

### Alberta Ecotrust — Climate Innovation Fund (Alberta Ecotrust Foundation)

- **URL:** https://albertaecotrust.com/grants/climate-innovation-grant
- **Eligibility:** Non-profit or qualified Canadian donee. **Geographic restriction: Calgary or Edmonton only** — fits an Edmonton-HQ'd CPD. Charitable status not required (non-charities accepted directly).
- **Award size:** Up to **$100,000** per project; $500,000 total per city per cycle.
- **Cycle:** EOI window **Jan 12 – Feb 20, 2026** (next cycle likely same window in 2027).
- **Fit:** Narrow but real: a focused deliverable — e.g., "tag and surface every Alberta-Hansard speech on climate / energy policy 2015–present; publish dataset; ship a public 'climate accountability tracker' UI on top of the existing CPD search" — fits "policy advancement + collective impact." Pre-application call with their staff strongly encouraged; the project must scale beyond one-off. Springboard Grant ($10K capacity-building) is a softer entry point.
- **Political-feasibility caveat: low.** Ecotrust is independent of the GoA, climate-frame is uncontroversial there.

### Alberta Foundation for the Arts — Community Focused Arts Organizations (AFA)

- **URL:** https://www.affta.ab.ca/funding/find-funding
- **Eligibility:** Alberta-based non-profit arts organization. Charitable status not required.
- **Award size:** Project funding up to **$18,000** for visual arts / new media; operational funding for established arts orgs varies.
- **Cycle:** Multiple program-specific deadlines through the year — verify on AFA portal.
- **Fit:** Weak as a primary funder. Could fit a single deliverable framed as **new-media / data-art**: e.g., a public installation, exhibition, or interactive data-art piece built on the Hansard corpus. Skip unless you have an artist collaborator and a clear cultural-output deliverable.

### Suncor Energy Foundation — Community Investment

- **URL:** https://www.suncor.com/en-ca/sustainability/community-investment/apply-for-funding
- **Eligibility:** **Registered charity required.** Three priority areas: Indigenous, community, energy. Cannot fund >40% of an org's annual operating costs; cannot fund orgs sitting on net-asset reserves > annual expenses.
- **Award size:** Variable; typically mid-five to low-six figures for community-investment recipients.
- **Cycle:** Rolling. Submit a 200-word preliminary request; Suncor reaches out if interested.
- **Fit:** Weak-to-zero. The "energy" priority is industry-aligned; a transparency project surfacing energy-policy speeches is exactly the kind of work an oil-major foundation will be cool on. **Recommend skipping.**

## Quick wins (apply now)

1. **Max Bell Foundation** — best topical match, rolling LOIs, no GoA political risk. Blocked only by charitable-status gate; pursue T3010 in parallel and/or find a fiscal-sponsor charity. If you can prep an LOI by July, you're in for the **September 2026 board** meeting.
2. **Alberta Ecotrust Climate Innovation** — 2026 EOI window has closed, but the Springboard Grant ($10K) is a low-friction entry to start a relationship before the **Jan–Feb 2027** main cycle. Edmonton-HQ qualifies you directly.
3. **CIP Project-Based** — three 2026 intakes left (May 15, Sept 15, plus Jan 15, 2027). Up to $75K, no charity status required, no partner required. The 1:1 match is the real friction; in-kind staff time counts.

## Watch list (track, don't apply yet)

- **CIP Operating Grant** — currently "under review"; sign up for Alberta.ca Community Grants notifications. Up to $75K *operating* funding would be transformative once it returns, though restored eligibility will likely tilt to UCP-priority sectors (food security / shelter / addictions).
- **Alberta Civil Society Fund** — last guidelines from 2022; status unclear. Worth a check-in with Community & Social Services in Q3 2026.
- **Calgary Foundation Community Grants** — apply once you have charitable status *and* a clear Treaty-7-region deliverable. Late-July 2026 intake.
- **AREF Investment Grant (August 2026 deadline)** — viable only if you scope a real-estate / infrastructure-sovereignty-flavoured deliverable.

## Political feasibility note (full)

UCP messaging since 2023 has been hostile to "democratic-engagement" framing it associates with federal Liberal initiatives, and the May 2025 CIP reallocation explicitly redirected funds toward UCP-priority social-services categories. CIP applications should lead with neutral civic-infrastructure language ("Albertans accessing the public record of their legislature," "open-data publication," "accessibility") — not "transparency / accountability / progressive civic-tech." Avoid framing CPD as scrutinising the current government in any GoA-funded application. If reviewer notes return that read as politically motivated, do not appeal — pivot to a different funder.

The non-government Alberta funders (Max Bell, AREF, Calgary Foundation, Ecotrust) carry no such risk and are the safer primary targets. Max Bell in particular *expects* policy-engagement framing; AREF and Ecotrust are issue-scoped (real-estate, climate) and apolitical in administration.
